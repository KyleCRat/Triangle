A Brief History of Triangle National


Triangle Fraternity was founded by 16 Civil Engineering students at the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champagne, and was incorporated on April 15, 1907. Triangle became a national fraternity when a second chapter was installed at Purdue on April 8, 1909. Initially, its membership was limited to civil engineering, but soon expanded to all engineering and architecture. Over the years, several science majors have been added to provide the mix that exists today. To date, over 23,000 members have been initiated through 45 chapters nationwide, with an alumni base that includes CEOs, Astronauts, Patent Holders, Governors, Olympic Gold Medal Winners, Explorers, and many others. Triangle is one of only three national fraternities to enjoy such a distinctive, non-Greek letter name, and is the only national fraternity to be selective in membership by academic concentration. Triangle has always been an organization that promotes balance between academic, social, and philanthropic excellence.




What is Triangle Fraternity?


Triangle is a "home," a family-like atmosphere. The word "fraternity" implies Brotherhood, a close group of friends sharing common interests that live together. Triangle provides an excellent place for students in technical fields to satisfy the many needs of college life. Triangle Fraternity is not your typical fraternity. We limit our memberships to those in the fields of engineering, sciences, and architecture. This makes us unique in the North-American Interfraternity Conference, being the only member fraternity that selects its membership from these majors. This is done to draw together outstanding men with similar course work and career goals
